Power-building:

and al little about my journey in it...

Intro and inspiration

Power-building is a combination of powerlifting and bodybuilding. it is essentially bodybuilding except done with heavy lifting. The best example of a power-builder is the 8 time consecutive Mr Olympia champion, Ronnie Coleman. He is regarded as the greatest bodybuilder of all time. However while his physique and sizing was monstrous, they were rivalled by the weight he pushed. This is the reason he is my inspiration.

My journey in Power-building

I began my journey in power-building about 2 years ago and have achieved great feats in both the strength and physique areas of the sport. I enjoy this sport because of both the heavy weight that can be pushed as well as the various health benefits that come with the sport. As a bonus there is also the fact that im always ready for summer. There are ups and downs in this sport and often times where theres no motivation to keep going but the continous progress and love for the sport is what has kept me going.

The bench, squat and deadlift

These 3 attributes are the only lifts in powerlifting and form a big part of any good workout schedule. This is because they are all compound lifts

Bench

The bench is always one of everyones favourite excercises and is the most commonly used compound lifts. It mainly activates the chest however it also uses the anterior deltoid and triceps to lift the weight. My current personal best in the bench press is 90 kgs, this is 10 kgs off my goal. The bench is used to achieve an overall bigger chest and can be done with different forms. This is my favourite lift to do and i strive to do it in a chest day at least 2 days per week.


Squat

While the squat is not everyones favourite and is often skipped, along with the rest of leg day, it is one of the most important lifts to getting bigger legs. However doing a squat correctly is also of high importance and should always be done either in a "parallel" manner. A squat activates the entirety of the lower body as well as the lower back muscle. Currently i have a personal record of 160 kg.

Deadlift

The deadlift is known as the king of compounds, this is due to the fact it activates every muscle in your body when performed. It is often the compound lift done with the most weight. On average the weight is double ones own bodyweight. This is the lift i am proudest of and can currently deadlift 210 kgs. While this weight is fun it is one of the more dangerous forms of lifts as it can cause both a hernia or lower back issues.
